# Dim 06 (Bass) — v1 tables summary

## bass_patterns.json
36 patterns expressed as chord-relative degree-rhythm grids (degree language: R, 3, b3, 5, b5, 6, b6, b7, 7, 8, 9, CH chromatic-approach, `.` rest, `~` tie). Each entry declares an archetype (root_pulse, alternating, octave, walking, funk, reggae, ska, latin, two_feel, shuffle, rock, arp, acid, 808, sub, reese, lilt, drone, none), mood-tag whitelist drawn only from M0..M10, density bin 0..5, supported time signatures, total `grid_cells` count (12 for 6/8 and shuffle, 16 elsewhere) with cell `len` values that sum exactly to `grid_cells`, articulation per cell (tenuto/staccato/legato/slide/ghost/dead), a ghost flag, a `chromatic_ok` flag gating CH approach tones, an `anticipation_cell` index (or null) marking the cell that crosses the next-chord boundary, and a register hint. Coverage: whole/half/quarter root pulses, root-fifth/country alternating, octave disco, walking diatonic and walking-jazz with chromatic, syncopated funk, dub offbeat, ska upstroke, dembow, latin tumbao, montuno, bossa, samba, jazz two-feel, Motown Jamerson walk, slap funk, shuffle blues, boogie-woogie, rock 8ths, rock gallop, metal pedal 16ths, synthwave arp, acid 303 16ths, trap 808 glide, dnb sub stab, dnb Reese, gospel walk-up, drone pedal, house pumping 8ths, half-note fifth walk, phrygian descending walk, 6/8 lilt, and an explicit `none_silent` entry for bass-off bars.

## bass_synths.json
9 synth profiles (sub_sine, fm_bass, electric_bass_pluck, upright_bass, synth_saw_low, acid_303, reese, slap_bass, trap_808) — slightly above the requested 8 to give every pattern at least one compatible source. Each entry names an `engine_hint` (surge_xt, dexed, sfizz) and `preset_hint`, mood-tag whitelist, MIDI octave window (e.g. 24-48 for sub-only sources, 28-55 for finger and slap basses), a `pattern_compat` list of pattern ids the source idiomatically supports, plus capability flags `supports_portamento` and `supports_filter_cc74`. Acid/Reese/808 turn portamento and filter modulation on; acoustic/finger basses leave them off. Octave windows respect Dim 06's "bass top ≤ melody bottom − M3" guardrail by capping aggressive walks at MIDI 55 and subs at 48.

## bass_articulation.json
6 articulation profiles (groove_tight, groove_loose, slap_funk, walking_jazz, dub_clean, sub_static) tying bass behaviour to the dim-10 humanization profile registry. Each carries `humanization_profile_ref` (groove-tight or groove-loose), a `ghost_velocity_max` (30–70) limiting how loud ghost-flagged cells can render, an `anticipation_velocity_boost` added to the cell at `anticipation_cell` to lean into the next chord, and a `default_release_pct_of_duration` (60 for slap, 100 for sub-static drone) that the renderer applies to MIDI note-off offsets. Slap_funk pushes ghost loud and release short; sub_static keeps gates pinned for drone/Reese parts; walking_jazz lifts anticipation cells modestly so chromatic approach notes punch through.

## Validation
All three files pass `python3 -m json.tool`. Pattern grid `len` sums exactly equal `grid_cells` for all 36 entries. All ids are unique lowercase_snake_case. All mood tags are within M0..M10. No leading `+N` numeric literals.
